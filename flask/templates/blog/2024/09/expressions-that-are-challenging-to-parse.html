An extension to example Latex expressions to parse

valid math latex in order of increasing complexity

A plain text symbol: $$ A $$ A symbol that uses Latex: $$ \vec{A} $$ operator with symbol $$ \lim_{x\to\infty} f(x) $$ operator with symbol $$ \sin x $$ operator between symbols $$ f \in g $$ An expression that has a left and right side: $$ A = B $$ An expression with multiple terms: $$ A + B = C $$ More complicated multi-term expressions that use Latex: $$ \vec{A} = u \hat{x} + v \hat{y} $$ $$ A^u + \frac{Y}{3} + \int B dx + \sum_{z=1}^{n} c_k = D f(y) - G|_0^{\infty} $$ An expression involving a matrix: $$ \hat{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3\\ a & b & c \end{bmatrix} $$ An expression with a condition: $$ A = x y \mbox{ when } x > 0 $$ Multi-part expression $$ f(x) = \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} x^2 & \mbox{ when } x \geq 0 \\ x & \mbox{ when } x < 0 \end{array} \right. $$ condition $$ A = x y \mbox{ where } x \in \mathbb{Z} $$ condition $$ A = x \mbox{ when } x \ll 3 $$ multiple expressions as one expression $$ x = \pm \sqrt{a^2 - 4} $$ non-equality relation operator $$ A \approx B $$ $$ A > B $$

See also https://www.cs.put.poznan.pl/ksiek/latexmath.html for examples.

Valid math Latex that is ambiguous

  • Does \(1/2\pi\) refer to \((1/2) \pi\) xor \(1/(2 \pi)\)?
  • Does \(\sin x / y\) refer to \( (\sin x)/y \) xor \( \sin (x/y) \)?
  • Does \(\sin x + 2\) refer to \( (\sin x) + 2 \) xor \( \sin (x + 2) \)?
